Coorg: Scotland of India

One of the most affluent hill stations of Karnataka. It is famous for its breathtaking greenery, mountain views and coffee plantations. It attracts tourists from all over the world because of its rich culture and people.

Coorg Cuisine

Bliss on a plate

Kodava culture and history have both greatly influenced their food habits. Food is an integral part of the Kodava culture and it is not taken lightly. The daily meal of a Kodava family is simple fare. The people of Coorg have been agriculturists from time immemorial. Their main occupation was paddy cultivation for a long time before coffee, spices and fruits were introduced.

So rice forms a major part of Coorg cuisine. They loved hunting game for sport and food and foraged for wild produce. Long before foraging became a cool thing to do, the Kodavas went foraging for bamboo, wild mushrooms, green leafy vegetables and fruits. Coorg cuisine is made up of seasonal produce and meat that is flavoured judiciously with spices but still retains its natural flavours.

Noolputtu

Noolputtu (string hoppers) are fine rice noodles made from a steamed dough of fine-grained broken rice and water. The dough is passed through a special noolputtu vara. A small plate is held underneath the press to catch the noodles. The plate is slowly turned around to ensure that the noodles have a nice circular shape.

Kadambuttu

The steamed rice dumpling called kadambuttu is one of the most famous dishes in Coorg cuisine. They are tiny white balls of buttery deliciousness that melt in your mouth. It is made with broken rice called kadumbuttu thari. The thari is cooked in boiling water with a small dollop of ghee, till it becomes a thick dough.

The cooked dough is kneaded till it’s smooth and rolled into small balls with a bit of butter.It is then steamed again till its cooked entirely.

Pandi Curry

Kachampuli is the concentrated extract of a fruit called panapuli.This extract gives the pandi curry its dark colour and its signature sour taste. The rich, dark, spicy curry is worthy of all the attention it gets as one of the most famous foods in Coorg.

Pandi curry goes very well with kadambuttu and akki otti.

Places to Visit

Your Adventure begins here

Tadiandamol Peak

The highest peak in the Coorg region and the second highest in Karnataka, Tadiyendamol is a trekker’s paradise. Located at an elevation of 1,748 m, the gentle slopes and grassy terrain lure you with their scenic charm and serene ambience.

Omkareshwara Temple

Dating back to 1820, the Omkareshwara Temple features Indo-Saracenic architecture with a central dome and turrets in four corners. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple attracts Hindu pilgrims and tourists alike for its religious significance as well as architectural brilliance.

Mallalli Falls

Though a seasonal waterfall, Mallalli Falls is definitely worth visiting if you’re planning a trip to Coorg during the rainy season. Cascading majestically from a height of over 200 feet, this waterfall located at the foothills of Pushpagiri mountain ranges counts among the tallest ones in South India.

Raja’s Tomb

Raja’s Tomb is the mausoleum of former Kodagu kings. The two identical tombs standing nearby belong to Veerajendra and his wife and date back to 1821. To their right is the tomb of Lingarajendra built in 1820, and the one on the left belongs to the royal priest Rudrappa and was built in 1834.

Coffee Plantations

Whiff in the fresh smell of coffee and tour the trails of coffee plantations at the Karagunda Coffee Plantation. It is located at an elevation 3,400 feet above the ground and is known for growing arabica coffee beans.

The beans are all hand-plucked by the locals once the coffee berry is riped. The displays extended by the coffee plantations are just beyond one’s imagination and one must not date to miss out on a chance to tour them.

Trekking

Mandalpatti Trek is located almost 25 km away from Madikeri and is known for its scenic views.The displays of the misty mountains, blankets of lush green shrubs, and exotic collection of flora and fauna, is what makes up the Mandalpatti Trek. If you are looking for an escape from the mundane schedules or just want to unwind along with the fresh breeze, this is the perfect place for you.

Camping

Camping in Coorg is one of the best recreational things to do when visiting this region as the soothing landscapes and serene ambiance of the place bring you close to the incredible nature and take you away from the city chaos.Camps like Jungle Mount Adventure Camp and Votecad Nature camp are known to offer some exciting adventurous activities such as jungle safaris and trekking, adding more fun to the whole camping experience.
